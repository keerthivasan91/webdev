ISRO Successfully Launches Chandrayaan-3, Aims for Lunar South Pole

India Becomes First Nation to Achieve Soft Landing Near Lunar South Pole

By Keerthi Vasan | February 18, 2025

India's Chandrayaan-3 mission has successfully achieved a soft landing near the Moon's south pole, marking a significant milestone in space exploration.

Mission Overview: Launched on July 14, 2023, from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Chandrayaan-3 comprises three main components:

  • Propulsion Module: Carried the lander and rover to a 100 km lunar orbit.
  • Lander Module (Vikram): Facilitated the soft landing on the Moon's surface.
  • Rover (Pragyan): Designed to explore the lunar surface and conduct scientific experiments.

Scientific Discoveries: The Pragyan rover's analysis of the lunar regolith revealed the presence of ferroan anorthosite, supporting the theory that the Moon was once covered by a vast "magma ocean."

India and Qatar Aim to Double Trade to $28 Billion in Five Years

India and Qatar have set a goal to double their trade volume to $28 billion over the next five years and are considering the signing of a free trade agreement.

This target was announced following discussions between Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Qatar's Emir Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al-Thani during the Emir's two-day official visit to New Delhi. In addition to trade, the two nations are looking to expand their energy cooperation.
